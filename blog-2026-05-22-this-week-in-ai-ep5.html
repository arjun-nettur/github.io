This Week in AI — Ep. 5: Google's Agent Gambit, Small Businesses Get Their Moment

Google launches Gemini Spark. Anthropic targets small businesses. And AI infrastructure reaches a breaking point.

NeeAr Ventures Editorial ◆ May 22, 2026 ◆ 7 min read ◆ AI Industry News

The AI industry just made three moves that define where this is actually going. Google shipped a 24/7 agent with a launch date—not a demo, not a roadmap, but actual beta access starting next week. Anthropic stopped chasing enterprise contracts and turned to small businesses. And behind all of it, a story no one wants to talk about: the U.S. is heading toward a 45-gigawatt power shortfall.

This isn't hype. This is infrastructure meeting reality. Here's what happened this week.

1 Google I/O 2026 — Gemini Goes All-In on Agents

Gemini Spark: The First Real 24/7 Agent

Google I/O happened this week, and the entire keynote was about one thing: Gemini isn't just an assistant anymore—it's becoming an agent.

The headline announcement: Gemini Spark, a 24/7 personal AI agent that lives in the Gemini app, Gmail, Google Workspace, Android, and iOS.

Here's what makes Spark different from every other "AI agent" demo we've seen:

Why Spark Matters
It's Not Vaporware

It has a launch date. Beta access starts next week for Google AI Ultra subscribers in the U.S. It's built on Gemini 3.5 Flash. Fast enough to be always-on, smart enough to handle complex tasks. It connects to your apps. MCP support is rolling out this summer, meaning Spark will work inside Canva, Instacart, OpenTable, and more. It's not just reactive. Spark is designed to proactively handle tasks—booking reservations, scheduling meetings, organizing your inbox—without you needing to prompt it every time.

The Competitive Context

OpenAI's Operator exists but remains limited. Anthropic has powerful agentic capabilities but they're spread across different tools. Google just shipped the first concrete 24/7 agent product with a demo, a date, and a distribution plan through the Gemini app. If Spark delivers on what Google showed at I/O, Google wins the agent category in 2026.

Gemini 3.5 Flash and Gemini Omni

Google also announced two new model families:

Gemini 3.5 Flash — The new foundation for everything Google AI. It combines frontier intelligence with the speed and cost of the Flash series. It's now live in the Gemini app, Google Search, Google's agent development platform Antigravity, and the Gemini API.

What's impressive: it surpasses Gemini 3.1 Pro in coding, agentic tasks, and multimodal benchmarks while running 4x faster than other frontier models in output tokens per second.

Gemini Omni — A new series of models that combines Gemini's reasoning with multimodal generation. It accepts image, audio, video, and text as input and outputs video grounded in real-world knowledge.

Think of it as Google's answer to video generation tools, but with Google's entire knowledge graph behind it.

2 Anthropic's Pivot — Claude for Small Business

The Big Bet on Small Business

On May 13, Anthropic launched Claude for Small Business—a package of connectors, ready-to-run workflows, and reusable skills designed to put Claude inside the tools small businesses already use.

The integrations at launch: Intuit QuickBooks, PayPal, HubSpot, Canva, DocuSign, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, and Slack.

15 Pre-Built Workflows
What Claude for Small Business Does

Payroll planning and 30-day cash forecasting · Month-end close and accountant packets · Chasing overdue invoices · Marketing campaigns (from HubSpot analysis to Canva assets) · Contract review before signing · Lead triage, margin analysis, tax prep · Employee onboarding · A "Monday morning business pulse" covering cash, pipeline, and commitments

The safety model: Users must initiate tasks and approve sensitive actions before Claude sends, posts, or pays anything.

Why This Matters

Most intensive AI adoption so far has happened at the enterprise level. Big companies with big budgets and big IT teams.

Small businesses—the ones running on QuickBooks, PayPal, and Google Workspace—have been left behind.

The Bet

The next wave of AI adoption won't come from Fortune 500 contracts. It'll come from solo entrepreneurs, mom-and-pop shops, and lean teams that can't afford to hire specialists but desperately need the leverage AI provides.

And there's a financial signal here: Anthropic's 2026 revenue run rate climbed above $30 billion, up from $9 billion last year. The number of customers spending $1 million annually doubled from 500 to over 1,000 in just two months.

This isn't a side project. This is Anthropic betting that small business is where AI becomes essential infrastructure.

The Tour and Training

Anthropic isn't just shipping software. They're taking Claude for Small Business on a 10-city U.S. tour starting May 14 in Chicago—free, half-day AI fluency training and hands-on workshops for 100 local small business leaders per stop.

The message: AI isn't just for tech companies anymore. It's for everyone. And we're going to show you how to use it.

3 The Infrastructure Question Everyone's Ignoring

The Power Problem

While Google and Anthropic are shipping agents and targeting new markets, there's a story underneath all of this that no one wants to talk about: AI infrastructure is hitting a wall.

The numbers:

  • AI-related capital expenditure is expected to surpass $750 billion in 2026
  • The U.S. faces a projected 45-gigawatt power shortfall for data centers by 2028
  • By 2030, we'll need 72 gigawatts of new capacity—the equivalent of 72 large nuclear power plants

The workforce gap:

  • Goldman Sachs estimates we'll need approximately 760,000 additional power and grid workers by 2030
  • That includes 207,000 specialized transmission and distribution roles that require three to four years of training to fill
  • That pipeline doesn't exist
What This Means

The AI labs are building faster than the grid can support them. Cloud costs are going up. Power availability is becoming a bottleneck. And the skilled workforce needed to build and maintain this infrastructure is years away.

What to do if you're building with AI:

  • Factor infrastructure risk into your long-term planning
  • Build efficiency into your AI workflows now
  • Don't assume cloud costs will stay flat or go down

This isn't a distant problem. This is happening now.

Quick Hits

Snap and Perplexity End $400M Partnership: Snap confirmed its planned $400 million partnership with Perplexity has ended. The deal would have integrated Perplexity's conversational AI search into Snapchat's chat interface, but the companies "amicably ended the relationship" during Q1 2026 before a broad rollout.

AI Search Changes SEO Forever: Traditional SEO dashboards are failing to capture how AI-generated answers are reshaping buyer behavior. According to data from 50 B2B SaaS keywords tracked in Q1 2026, the shift from clicks to influence is real—and marketers aren't tracking it yet.

Intelligent Eyewear Returns: Google and Samsung announced new "intelligent eyewear" devices launching this fall—Google's second attempt after Google Glass. The difference this time: conversational AI changes the use case from novelty to utility.

What This Week Tells Us

Three signals that matter:

1. Agents are shipping. Google isn't demoing the future—they're releasing a 24/7 agent next week with real integrations and a clear rollout plan.

2. Small businesses are the new frontier. Anthropic is betting that the next wave of AI adoption comes from solo entrepreneurs and lean teams, not enterprise contracts.

3. Infrastructure is the silent bottleneck. Behind every new model, every new agent, every new feature, there's a power grid and a workforce that can't keep up.

AI has crossed a threshold. It's not about what's coming anymore—it's about what's already here and who's moving fast enough to use it.
